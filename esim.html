eSIM is the new shift

Airtel

Recently, I read about eSIMs and how the Airtel CEO advocates for a shift to eSIM technology. He emphasized that in our digital-first lifestyles, where mobile devices, tablets, and smartwatches are interconnected, eSIMs provide seamless connectivity. He highlighted the advantages of eSIMs, such as enabling multiple user profiles with just one click, allowing users to essentially have 3 to 5 virtual SIMs simultaneously.

However, my concerns primarily revolve around the drawbacks of eSIMs. One issue mentioned was the need for a separate slot for eSIMs, preventing manufacturers from utilizing that space for improving battery capacity or incorporating faster CPUs. Additionally, the inability to physically swap a SIM in case of a phone crash poses a practical challenge. I recently experienced a situation where my phone crashed, and despite having a spare device, I couldn't use it because of the eSIM limitation. This has been a recurring issue within my family as well.

Security is another significant concern. While it's not very common for an entire phone to be hacked, the ease of exploiting a phone with an eSIM raises potential security risks. The lack of an option to physically track the SIM is a drawback, and having this feature could enhance security.

Currently, iPhones offer eSIM functionality, but the question arises: when will mid-range and lower-range smartphone companies adopt this feature? If this shift occurs, it's crucial to consider the technology implemented to ensure the security of the SIM and the associated data. Additionally, improvements in network speed and connectivity are anticipated, potentially enhancing the overall user experience. As we witness advancements in technology, particularly with eSIMs, it will be interesting to see how these developments unfold and what they hold for the future. Let's hope for the best.
